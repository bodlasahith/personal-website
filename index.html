Object Detection System for the Visually Impaired

This device provides the visually impaired with knowledge of their surroundings. Though the blind generally use canes to map out their path a few steps in front of themselves, this device corroborates some information gathered from touch using hearing. Just like how bats use echolocation to discover prey at night rather than vision, blind people tend to rely on sound to comprehend and acknowledge their surroundings when, for instance, crossing the street or walking through a mall.
